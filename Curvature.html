1. Background

2. What we're building to

  • The radius of curvature at a point on a curve is, loosely speaking, the radius of a circle which fits the curve most snugly at that point.
  • The curvature, denoted κ κ kappa\kappaκ, is one divided by the radius of curvature.
  • In formulas, curvature is defined as the magnitude of the derivative of a unit tangent vector function with respect to arc length:
κ = ∥ d T d s ∥ κ = d T d s kappa=||(dT)/(ds)||\kappa=\left\|\frac{d T}{d s}\right\|κ=∥dTds∥
Don't worry, I'll talk about each step of computing this value.
  • The intuition here is that the unit tangent vector tells you which direction you are moving, and the rate at which it changes with respect to small steps d s d s dsd sds along the curve is a good indication of how quickly you are turning.
